Director, FYROM/USA

Milo Manchevski is a Macedonian director, screenwriter, photographer and artist who lives and works in New York. His debut feature Pre Rain (Before the Rain, 1994) was nominated for the Oscar Award, and won the Golden Lion at the Venice Film Festival, as well as the FIPRESCI and Independent Spirit prizes and thirty other awards. The New York Times included this achievement on the list of "1000 Best Movies ever." recorded ". Jovan Jovanović

Director, Serbia

Born in Belgrade in 1940. He graduated from Directors at the Academy of Theater, Film, Radio and Television in the middle-length feature film Izrazito ja (1967), which was named the best film in all categories at the New York Festival of Young Authors. His short documentary film Kolt 15 Gap (1971) was awarded at festivals in Oberhausen, Utrecht, Belgrade. The most important Jovanovic's documentaries are: The Running Revolution (1972), Working People Artists (1975), Narcotics (1976), The Man Who Made Systems (1990), Vukovar Why (1991). His most important feature films are Express Me (1969), Young and Healthy Rose (1971), Landscape in the Fog (1984).

Mila Turajlić

Director, Serbia

Mila Turajlić was born on July 4, 1979 in Belgrade. She studied at the London School of Economics, as well as at the FDU in Belgrade. She has gained experience working on films such as Apocalypto, Fade to Black and Brothers Bloom, and since 2005 she has been a producer of the documentary film Seven Magnificent in Belgrade. Her debut documentary Cinema Komunisto was premiered at the International Documentary Filmfestival Amsterdam 2010, and won 16 awards around the world, including the Golden Hugo prize at the Chicago Film Festival. Her second feature-length documentary, The Other Side of Everything (2017), was screened at the TIFF Film Festival in Toronto, and at the IDFA Festival she won the Best Feature Film Award.
